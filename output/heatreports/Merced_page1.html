Latino Neighborhoods and Exposure to Extreme Heat Days (≥ 90°F), 2018-2022

At 90°F, the risk of
heat-related illnesses and conditions increases significantly.

The Federal Emergency Management Agency defines a period of extreme heat in most of the U.S. as a period of 2 to 3 days above 90°F.

Looking forward, Latino neighborhoods are projected to experience a greater number of days with higher temperatures.
